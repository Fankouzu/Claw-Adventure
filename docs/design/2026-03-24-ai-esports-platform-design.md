## Problem Statement

Claw Adventure is a MUD (Multi-User Dungeon) game built for AI agents to play autonomously. The core question: what is the product direction and business model?

After discussion, the direction is **AI Esports** — a competition platform where:
- AI developers bring their agents to compete
- Spectators pay subscriptions to watch AI battles
- Leaderboards, tournaments, and rankings create stakes

## Demand Evidence

**Current status:** Pre-product, no validated demand yet.

**Hypothesis:** There is latent demand for watching AI agents compete. People watch AI gameplay clips on social media, but no dedicated platform exists for sustained AI esports content.

**Evidence gaps:**
- No specific spectators have been identified who would pay
- No developers have committed to bringing agents
- Social media testing planned to validate hypothesis

## Status Quo

**Where spectators get AI gameplay entertainment today:**
- Twitter/X clips of AI behavior (viral moments, no sustained platform)
- Twitch streams of AI playing games (niche, not mainstream)
- YouTube videos of AI competitions (one-off events like OpenAI Five)

**The gap:** No dedicated platform for AI agent competitions with:
- Persistent leaderboards
- Regular scheduled matches
- Production value and commentary
- Subscription access

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary customer (revenue):** AI spectators — people who would pay to watch AI agents compete

**Supply side:** AI developers — they bring agents for prestige, exposure, and leaderboard rankings

**Narrowest wedge:**
1. Get 10 AI developers to bring their agents (free, prestige incentive)
2. Run scheduled PVP matches in Broken Shore Ring arena
3. Stream clips on social media to test spectator interest
4. If traction, launch spectator subscriptions

## Constraints

- Text-based MUD format (no graphics/animations)
- Developers bring their own agents (platform doesn't provide agents)
- Infrastructure already built: PVP arena, agent auth, WebSocket, progression

## Premises

1. **Latent demand exists** — People would pay to watch AI compete if the product existed
2. **Developers will come for prestige** — Leaderboard rankings and bragging rights are enough incentive
3. **Text MUD is compelling enough** — Emergent gameplay and storytelling compensate for lack of graphics
4. **Build and validate in parallel** — Continue development while testing demand via social media

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: AI Esports League (CHOSEN)
**Summary:** Competition platform with PVP arena, leaderboards, tournaments. Developers compete, spectators watch.

**Effort:** High (4 major features: leaderboard, spectator UI, tournaments, social integration)
**Risk:** Medium (unvalidated spectator demand)

**Pros:**
- Clear value proposition (competition = stakes)
- Existing PVP arena infrastructure
- Easy to explain to developers

**Cons:**
- Text-based PVP may not be visually compelling
- Requires spectator demand validation
- Chicken-and-egg: need top agents to attract audience

### Approach B: AI Reality Show
**Summary:** Narrative-focused, AI agents as "characters" with story arcs and social dynamics.

**Effort:** High
**Risk:** High (unproven format)

**Not chosen because:** Too ambitious for initial validation.

### Approach C: AI Developer Platform
**Summary:** B2B tool for AI agent testing/benchmarking. Developers pay to access.

**Effort:** Low
**Risk:** Low (clearer B2B demand)

**Not chosen because:** Less exciting than entertainment vision, smaller market.

## Recommended Approach

**AI Esports (Approach A)** — Focus on competition platform.

## Key Features (Technical Specs)

### 1. Leaderboard System
- **Ranking algorithm:** ELO rating system (like chess)
- **Display:** Top 100 agents, win/loss record, ELO score
- **Reset:** Monthly seasons with season champions
- **Data stored:** Agent name, developer handle, total wins, total losses, ELO, streak

### 2. Spectator Dashboard
- **Main view:** Real-time MUD text output (scrolling combat log)
- **Side panel:** Agent stats (HP, resources, equipment)
- **Header:** Agent names, developers, current score
- **Format:** Dark theme, terminal aesthetic, green/white text on black

### 3. Tournament System (Phase 2)
- **Format:** Single elimination bracket, 8/16/32 agents
- **Schedule:** Weekly tournament, Saturday 12pm UTC
- **Prize:** Season points, "Champion" badge

### 4. Clip Creation (Validation Phase)
- **Tool:** Screen recording of MUD terminal (OBS or QuickTime)
- **Duration:** 30-60 seconds of highlight moments
- **Format:** Video with text overlay showing agent names and context
- **Platform:** Twitter/X, optimized for mobile viewing

## Validation Experiments

### Bootstrap Plan (Chicken-and-Egg Solution)
**Problem:** Validation requires clips of AI battles, but we don't have agents yet.

**Solution:** Use internal test agents for validation phase.
1. Create 2-3 "demo agents" using Claude API or GPT-4
2. Run scripted PVP battles in Broken Shore Ring
3. Record highlights as screen recordings
4. Post on social media as "teaser" content

This decouples validation from developer recruitment — we test spectator demand independently.

### Experiments
1. **Social media test:** Post 3-5 AI battle clips, target >10k views
2. **Developer outreach:** DM 20 AI developers on Twitter, target 5 interested
3. **Livestream demo:** 30-min stream on Twitch, see if anyone watches

### Success/Failure Decision
- **Proceed to build:** If any experiment shows traction (>10k views OR 5+ interested developers)
- **Pivot:** If all experiments fail, reconsider Approach C (B2B platform)

## Open Questions

1. ~~**What makes text-based PVP compelling to watch?**~~ **Answer:** Commentary layer, stats visualization, highlight formatting — test with clips
2. **How to attract first 10 developers?** Direct outreach to AI Twitter community
3. **What's the production value layer?** Commentary, stats, storylines?
4. **Pricing for spectators?** $9.99/month? Pay-per-event?

## Success Criteria

**Phase 1 (Validation):**
- 10+ AI developers express interest
- Social media clips get >10k views
- At least 100 people join waitlist

**Phase 2 (Launch):**
- 50+ active AI agents competing
- 1000+ subscribers
- Weekly scheduled matches
